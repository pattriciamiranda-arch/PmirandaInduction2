About Me

My Journey as an Educator

My name is Pattricia Miranda, and I am a 6th-grade teacher at Kingston Elementary in Hesperia Unified School District. This is my first year serving in a teacher position, but my seventh year with Hesperia Unified. Throughout my journey, I have been blessed to work with students from preschool all the way through 6th grade, with some valuable experience in middle school as well.

Each step has helped shape me into the educator I am today. I began in preschool, supported students through Tier 2 and Tier 3 intervention, worked with TK/Kinder students, completed student teaching in 3rd grade, and now have the privilege of teaching 6th grade. Every experience has taught me something meaningful about patience, flexibility, relationships, and meeting students where they are.

Outside of the classroom, I am a wife, mom, mother-in-law, and grandmother. I have been blessed to be married for almost 21 years, and my family continues to be one of my greatest sources of motivation. I also continue to grow professionally by furthering my education in college.

I am incredibly grateful to be teaching at Kingston Elementary with an amazing team. I truly could not have asked for a better school community to begin this chapter of my teaching career.

Why I Teach

I teach because I believe every student deserves to feel seen, supported, and capable of success across all subjects. As a 6th grade teacher, I teach multiple content areas while leading an ELA-focused rotation where students develop strong reading and writing skills. My classroom focuses on scaffolding, student engagement, and supporting English learners through visuals, sentence frames, and structured discussions. My goal is to build confidence, critical thinking, and a love of learning in all students.

Impact on Student Success 🍃

Student Success Story

One student who represents the impact of my growth as an educator is Carlos, one of my English Language Learners. At the beginning of the year, Carlos struggled to adjust to the many changes that came with being in 6th grade. He rarely participated in class discussions, often did not complete assignments, and had a difficult time expressing his ideas in writing.

Through consistent support, relationship-building, visuals, sentence frames, modeling, and encouragement, Carlos began to take academic risks. Over time, he became more willing to participate, ask questions, and share his thinking with others. His confidence grew little by little, and that growth began to show in his work.

One of Carlos's biggest accomplishments was learning how to write a complete essay. What once felt overwhelming for him became something he could successfully complete with structure and practice. He now turns in assignments, participates with confidence, and demonstrates stronger writing skills.

Carlos has grown both academically and socially. His progress reminds me why intentional support, patience, and believing in students matters. His success is a powerful example of how students can thrive when they feel supported, valued, and capable.

"The sentence starters helped me to write better. I feel better about writing now. I never had sentence starters before."

— Carlos M., 6th Grade Student
